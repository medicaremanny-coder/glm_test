## 👤 Profile

> [!info]+ Who was Jim Rohn?
> **Emanuel James "Jim" Rohn** (September 17, 1930 – December 5, 2009) was an American entrepreneur, author, and motivational speaker — widely called **"America's Foremost Business Philosopher."** For over **40 years** he shared his philosophy of personal development with more than **6,000 audiences and 5 million people worldwide**.
>
> Raised on a farm in **Caldwell, Idaho**, he dropped out of college after one year and was broke at 25. Then he met his mentor, **[[Earl Shoaff]]**, who told him: *"If you want to be wealthy and happy, learn this lesson well: Learn to work harder on yourself than you do on your job."* By 31, Rohn was a millionaire.

## 🧠 Core Philosophy — The Rohn Operating System

> [!abstract]- 1. Work harder on yourself than on your job
> Your income rarely exceeds your personal development. Success is not something you pursue — it's something you **attract** by the person you become. *"Learn to work harder on yourself than you do on your job. If you work hard on your job you'll make a living; if you work hard on yourself you can make a fortune."*

> [!abstract]- 2. The same wind blows on us all
> *"It's the set of the sail, not the direction of the wind, that determines which way we will go."* Economic winds, political winds, social winds — they blow on everyone. The difference in arrival is the **set of the sail** (your philosophy).

> [!abstract]- 3. Discipline vs. Regret
> *"We must all suffer from one of two pains: the pain of discipline or the pain of regret. The difference is discipline weighs ounces while regret weighs tons."*

> [!abstract]- 4. The Seasons of Life
> Life and business are like the seasons. You can't change the seasons, but you can change **yourself**. Learn to handle the winters, take advantage of the springs, nourish and protect in summer, and reap without complaint in the fall.

> [!abstract]- 5. Profits are better than wages
> Wages make you a living — **profits make you a fortune**. Even part-time, find a way to earn profit: it changes your economics *and* your psychology.

> [!abstract]- 6. The Average of Five
> **"You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with."** Audit your associations: Who am I around? What do they have me doing, reading, saying? Is that OK?
